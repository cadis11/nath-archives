The Nath Tradition

The Nath Sampradaya is one of the oldest and most profound spiritual lineages of India. Founded in the mystical teachings of Adi Nath (Shiva himself), carried through Matsyendranath and perfected by the immortal Gorakhnath, this tradition encompasses Hatha Yoga, Kundalini Shakti, and the path of the Siddhas.

Adi Nath — Lord Shiva

The primordial Guru of the Nath lineage. Shiva as Adi Nath transmitted the yogic sciences to Matsyendranath in the depths of the ocean.

Matsyendranath

The great Siddha who received Shiva's teachings and transmitted them to Gorakhnath. He is revered as one of the Nine Naths and the founding Guru of Hatha Yoga.

Gorakhnath

The immortal Mahayogi who systematized the Nath tradition. Author of numerous yogic texts, he is worshipped as a deity across Nepal, India, and beyond.

The Nine Naths

Navnath — the nine great Siddhas of the tradition: Macchindranath, Gorakshanath, Jalandharnath, Kanifnath, Gahininath, Bhartrinath, Revananath, Charpatinath, Naganath.

84 Siddhas

The Chaurasi Siddhas — the 84 great accomplished masters of the Nath-Tantric tradition who achieved liberation through yogic practice.

Living Tradition

The Nath tradition continues today through Gorakhpur's famous Gorakhnath temple, the Kanphata Yogis, and countless practitioners across South Asia.
